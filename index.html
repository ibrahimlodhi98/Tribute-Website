Dr Abdul Qadeer Khan

Mohsin-e-Pakistan

Khan Research Laboratories and atomic bomb program

In April 1976, Khan joined the atomic bomb program and became part of the enrichment division, initially collaborating with Khalil Qureshi – a physical chemist.[56]: 62–63  Calculations performed by him were valuable contributions to centrifuges and a vital link to nuclear weapon research, but continue to push for his ideas for feasibility of weapon-grade uranium even though it had a low priority, with most efforts still aimed to produce military-grade plutonium.[53]: 73–74 [56] Because of his interest in uranium metallurgy and his frustration at having been passed over for director of the uranium division (the job was instead given to Bashiruddin Mahmood), Khan refused to engage in further calculations and caused tensions with other researchers.[40]: 147–148  Khan became highly unsatisfied and bored with the research led by Mahmood – finally, he submitted a critical report to Bhutto, in which he explained that the "enrichment program" was nowhere near success.

Biography

  • Full Name: Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan
  • Born: 1 April 1936, Bhopal, British India
  • Died: 10 October 2021, Islamabad, Pakistan
  • Nationality: Pakistani
  • He migrated to Pakistan in 1952 after the creation of Pakistan.
  • He completed his early education in Karachi.
  • He earned higher education from Europe, including degrees in metallurgical engineering.
  • He is known as the “Father of Pakistan’s Nuclear Program.”
